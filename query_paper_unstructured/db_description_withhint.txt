HINTS:
- The paper title in the Citations SQLite table matches the filename (without .txt extension) of documents in MongoDB.
- Common venues include: "CHI", "Ubicomp", "CSCW", "DIS", "PervasiveHealth", "WWW", "IUI", "OzCHI", "TEI", "AH".
- Common sources (publishers) include: "ACM", "IEEE", "PubMed".
- Common domains include: "food", "physical activity", "sleep", "mental", "finances", "productivity", "screen time", "social interactions", "location", "chronic", "diabetes", "health_behavior".
- Contribution types include: "empirical", "artifact", "theoretical", "survey", "methodological".
- Fields like domain, source, venue, and contribution may contain multiple values (e.g., a paper can be in multiple domains or have multiple contribution types).
- The citation_year in the Citations table represents when citations were received, which is after the paper's publication year.
- When matching domains or contributions, use substring/contains matching since values may be part of a list.
